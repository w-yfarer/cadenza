Racial outline: The origins of the Melde have been romanticised through time – a fairy tale turned to life. It goes like this: a blessing, a red string tied to the last finger of their hand, and intertwined destinies. In reality, there’s no string, but a sense of something missing, a constant yearning to seek out this person that’s meant to you whether it be romantic or in a completely platonic sense. Some are born lucky to have their soulmates be their siblings, a half of another whole that’s quickly reunited, but others are left to wander, oftentimes making Meldes nomadic and constantly traveling.

The first time Meldes come into physical contact with their other half – there is a shift, as if the heavens themselves have moved, and in that very instant, a connection is created. Their destinies are forever intertwined, an invisible string of fate, two halves of a whole coming to be. There are stories of what happens after as well, how Melde will appear when their soulmate is in trouble, how they always seem to know. And for all the happy endings, there are tragedies – tales of soulmates taken away too soon and the dangers of seeing the one you love die before your eyes. They’re cautionary tales to the younger Melde: you are never meant to be apart once you’ve found them, distance may breed fondness but also heartache.

Age span: Inherits the age span of their soulmate’s race and will live it out regardless or not if their soulmate prematurely dies. However, if the race is immortal, Meldes will die the moment their soulmates die (e.g. if one’s soulmate is a phoenix, a Melde will die upon the first death of their soulmate, and sever the link).

Physical characteristics: Humanoid in appearance, although they may take small traits from their non-Melde parent.

Special abilities: Melde have an inherent knowledge of what their soulmate is feeling at any given time, however the strength of the link varies – it grows in power once a bond is established to the point that the emotional link can give them knowledge of where their other half is at any given time (the accuracy and specific location is improved by being in closer proximity to their soulmate). While they do not inherit any racial traits of their secondary parent, instead, they gain a racial trait of their soulmate, albeit somewhat weakened *. In a sense, their existence revolves around finding their second half, and the world gives them as many clues as it can to help find them.

* Please note that Melde are unable to inherit powers such as forming demon pacts, a phoenix’s ability to reincarnate, and so forth. Rather they inherit non-racial specific powers, such as a kitsune’s ability to manipulate fire or an angel’s ability to wield light.

Weaknesses: Meldes are like humans in the way that they’re not impervious to harm, furthermore once a link is established, their racial abilities weaken the longer they are away from their soulmates. There is also an innate sense to keep their other halves from harm, and seeing them in such situations can cause them to be tunneled-vision to the point that they ignore the safety of their own well-being. Sometimes, prolonged distance from a soulmate - or knowledge of who the soulmate is, but being unable to be with them - can drive one to near-insanity. As previous, their lifespan is linked to their soulmates - if their soulmate dies prematurely, a Melde is forced to live out the rest of their lifespan with a severed link, a consistent ghost pain - and if their soulmate is immortal, and dies, then a Melde will die with them, regardless of where they are at the time.
